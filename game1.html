  • Right to Safety
  • Right to Information
  • Right to Choose
  • Right to Redressal
  • Right to Consumer Education
  • Consumers have the right to choose from a variety of goods and services at competitive prices, ensuring freedom in purchasing decisions.
  • Consumers have the right to seek redress against unfair or restrictive trade practices and can claim compensation for faulty goods or services.
  • Consumers have the right to be informed about the quality, quantity, price, and purity of goods and services to avoid unfair trade practices.
  • Consumers have the right to acquire knowledge and skills to be informed and responsible buyers through consumer education programs.
  • Consumers have the right to be protected against the marketing of goods and services that are hazardous to life and property. This applies especially to products like food, pharmaceuticals, and electrical appliances.
