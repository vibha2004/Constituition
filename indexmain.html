Child Labor Laws

The Child Labour (Prohibition and Regulation) Act, 1986 (amended in 2016)

  • What it is: Prohibits the employment of children below 14 years in hazardous occupations and regulates the conditions of work for children above 14 years.
  • Purpose: To eliminate child labor in hazardous industries and ensure children above 14 years are employed under regulated conditions.
  • Significance: Establishes clear guidelines for what constitutes hazardous work and ensures safe working conditions for children who are legally allowed to work.

The Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act, 2009 (RTE Act)

  • What it is: Guarantees free and compulsory education for children aged 6 to 14 years.
  • Purpose: Ensures that every child in this age group receives an education and reduces the need for child labor by making education a right.
  • Significance: Supports the goal of eliminating child labor by making education accessible and mandatory, thereby encouraging children to attend school rather than work.

The Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015

  • What it is: Provides a comprehensive framework for the care and protection of children in need of protection and rehabilitation, including those involved in child labor.
  • Purpose: Ensures that children involved in labor are provided with appropriate care, protection, and rehabilitation services.
  • Significance: Focuses on the welfare of children who are at risk of exploitation and provides mechanisms for their rehabilitation and reintegration into society.

The Factories Act, 1948

  • What it is: Regulates the working conditions in factories, including provisions related to child labor.
  • Purpose: Restricts the employment of children under 14 years in factories and sets conditions for the employment of adolescents.
  • Significance: Ensures that factories adhere to safety and health standards, particularly concerning the employment of younger workers.

The Mines Act, 1952

  • What it is: Prohibits the employment of children under 18 years in mines.
  • Purpose: Protects children from the hazardous conditions prevalent in mining activities.
  • Significance: Ensures that children are not exposed to dangerous working conditions in the mining industry.
